Reading the Trap List

Name: The name as given in the game's lists.

Type: The placement position of the trap; also used as categorisation by the game. Generally, a Ceiling trap and a Floor trap cannot occupy the same space, but a Wall Trap can cover the same squares as a Ceiling or Floor trap, which is important for comboing.

Align: Which daemon the trap is aligned with. This is important only for how XP is allocated, or for missions that require a minimum number of points or that you avoid a particular trap alignment. Generally, Sadistic traps do more damage, Humiliating traps have more utility features, and Elaborate traps balance the two.

Damage: The base damage done by the trap.

Ark: The trap's Ark multiplier, which affects your score. In Blood Ties, it also affects how much Warl you earn.

Time: The time the trap takes to reset.

XP: The base XP award for hitting with the trap.

Traits: The traits or types of the trap. This is mainly used for determining if enemies will resist the trap or not. Some traps have no Traits in which case the trap cannot be resisted.

XP/Warl Cost: The necessary amount of XP (with the appropriate daemon) and Warl needed to purchase the trap in Blood Ties. XP is not actually spent when you buy a trap, but Warl is. In Nightmare Princess, all traps are obtained as Nightmare Fruit, so traps unique to Nightmare Princess have no XP or Warl cost.

Effect: Most traps have one or both of two common effects: they deal damage and/or force the opponent to move. This section provides a brief description of the unusual effects of the trap. The terms used are as follows:

  • Physics: most Boulder-type traps are affected by physics after activation and can roll down stairs, be hit by Springboards, etc.
  • Wander X: the enemy is forced to move X squares forward under their own steam, usually unable to see.
  • Launch X: the enemy is thrown X squares in a parabolic arc.
  • Push X: the enemy is moved X squares along the ground and the trap moves with them.
  • Pull X: the enemy is moved X squares along the ground into the trap.
  • Facing: the enemy is moved in the direction they are facing after the trap's Facing effect (below) has taken effect.
  • Fixed: the enemy is moved in the direction the trap faces, or in the case of Pull, towards the trap.
  • Persists: the trap hangs around for a bit after hitting, potentially hitting multiple times.
  • Area X: the trap covers more than 1 square. When using traps with Area that also force movement, remember that the arrow on the trap setup screen shows where the enemy will be moved if they are hit at the centre of the area. If the enemy is hit at the edge (which they usually will be if the trap is part of a combo) their end position will be adjusted.
  • Missile: the trap launches a missile along a line of the map. The missile continues until it strikes something.
  • Hold: the trap holds the enemy in place for a while.
  • Ignites: the trap sets the enemy on fire. This will cause them to take continuous damage and to run randomly at double speed, unable to take actions, for a while. This same effect applies to you if you are somehow caught in the trap!
  • Enrage: the trap enrages the enemy. This will cause them to run at double speed directly towards the player and attempt to attack her in close melee, even if they would normally prefer to shoot or otherwise are not usually a melee type enemy. The game's tutorial states that they also ignore stage traps, but while this is true it isn't the free pass it sounds like, because they do still pathfind: they won't (for example) run into a buzzsaw on the stage, because doing so would provide no route to you. It may be possible to lure them into proximity sensor traps that are not obstacles, and it is certainly possible to lure them into placed traps and moving trapmobiles. If your character is hit by an enraging effect, she'll move continously at double speed and be unable to take actions, but you can still control her.

Facing: Apart from position there are two other key elements to whether or not a trap will combo: which way the enemy is facing, and whether they are lying on the floor (down) or standing up (up). This section shows you the effect the trap has on these. The first section shows which up/down state the enemy is required to be in to be hit with the trap, and which state they will be in after the trap is completed. For example, "Any>Down" means that the trap will hit an enemy who is standing up or lying down, and after being hit, they will be lying down. The second section shows the effect the trap has on the enemy's facing; "Same" means that the enemy's facing is unchanged. "Force" means that the enemy is forced to face the same way as the trap. "NoReverse" is the most common: it means that the enemy is forced to face the same way as the trap UNLESS they are currently facing the opposite way, in which case their facing is unchanged.

The "starred" versions of traps are not shown since they usually have an additional effect such as giving extra Ark or XP, rather than an effect that makes a difference to how you use the trap.
